Building a portfolio is one of those things every developer puts off. There's always something more "important" to work on. But I realized something: your portfolio is a reflection of how you think about craft.

## The Template Problem

Most developer portfolios look the same. Same hero sections. Same "I build scalable applications" copy. Same animated gradient backgrounds that scream "I watched too many tutorials."

Nothing wrong with templates - they're practical. But there's a difference between using a tool and understanding how it works.

## What I Learned

Building this portfolio taught me things no tutorial could:

- **CSS is deeper than you think** - Masking, blend modes, custom properties
- **Performance is a feature** - Every animation has a cost
- **Simplicity is hard** - Removing things is harder than adding them

## The Brutalist Approach

I chose a brutalist aesthetic because it aligns with my philosophy: communicate clearly, don't decorate unnecessarily. The pixel background isn't a gimmick - it's texture. The mono font isn't nostalgia - it's precision.

---

Your portfolio doesn't need to impress recruiters. It needs to tell them who you are.
